Rules:

Any live cell with fewer than two live neighbours dies, as if by underpopulation.


Any live cell with two or three live neighbours lives on to the next generation.


Any live cell with more than three live neighbours dies, as if by overpopulation.


Any dead cell with exactly three live neighbours becomes a live cell, as if by reproduction.

Video shuffler

The project involved creating a video player that shuffles the frames of a video to create a new, unique frame and therefor avoid copyright infringement. This was achieved by breaking the video down into triangle, shuffling them randomly, and then reassembling them into a new frame using webgl and webcodecs. However, after further research ,I discovered that shuffling frames is not a legal method to avoid copyright infringement, and using copyrighted content without permission is against the law. Additionally, technical limitations such as CORS and how h264 compression worked made it very hard to continue the project
